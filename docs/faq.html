🚀 Getting Started

What is Lendefi Markets?

Lendefi Markets is a composable lending protocol that creates isolated markets for each base asset (USDC, USD1, USDT, etc.). Each market operates independently with its own liquidity pools, collateral assets, and risk parameters.

Key features include:

  • Isolated lending markets per base asset
  • ERC-4626 compliant vaults for liquidity providers
  • Individual position vaults for complete asset segregation
  • Cross-collateral and isolated collateral modes
  • Flash loans with configurable fees
  • Automated liquidations and MEV protection
How do I get started as a lender?

To start earning yield as a lender:

  • Connect your wallet to the Lendefi Markets app
  • Choose a market (USDC, USD1, USDT, etc.)
  • Deposit assets into the market's ERC-4626 vault
  • Receive vault shares that represent your liquidity position
  • Earn yield from borrower interest, flash loan fees, and liquidation proceeds

You can withdraw your liquidity anytime, subject to available liquidity in the vault.

How do I start borrowing?

To borrow against your collateral:

  • Connect your wallet and choose a base asset market
  • Create a position (this deploys your individual vault)
  • Supply collateral to your position vault
  • Borrow base assets up to your position's credit limit
  • Monitor your health factor to avoid liquidation
⚠️ Important: Keep your health factor above 1.0 to avoid liquidation. Consider maintaining it above 1.5 for safety.
What networks are supported?

Lendefi Markets is currently deployed on:

  • Ethereum Mainnet - Production deployment
  • Sepolia Testnet - Testing and development

Additional networks may be supported in the future based on community demand and technical requirements.

💰 Markets & Assets

What's the difference between isolated and cross-collateral markets?

Cross-Collateral Mode:

  • Use multiple collateral assets in one position
  • Higher borrowing capacity
  • Suitable for established assets (WETH, WBTC, major DeFi tokens)
  • Shared liquidation risk across all collateral

Isolated Mode:

  • Single collateral asset per position
  • Limited borrowing capacity (debt caps)
  • Ideal for long-tail or newer assets
  • Risk contained to individual positions
What are collateral tiers?

Lendefi Markets uses four collateral tiers with different risk parameters:

  • STABLE (Tier 0): Stablecoins - Lowest fees, highest LTV
  • CROSS_A (Tier 1): Blue-chip assets (WETH, WBTC) - Low fees
  • CROSS_B (Tier 2): Established DeFi tokens - Medium fees
  • ISOLATED (Tier 3): Long-tail assets - Higher fees, debt caps

Higher tiers have increased borrow rates and liquidation fees to compensate for additional risk.

How are interest rates determined?

Interest rates are dynamic and based on:

  • Utilization Rate: Higher utilization = higher rates
  • Collateral Tier: Riskier collateral = higher rates
  • Base Rate: Minimum rate for all borrowing
  • Jump Rate: Additional rate based on collateral tier

Formula: Borrow Rate = Base Rate + (Utilization × Multiplier) + Tier Jump Rate

Supply Rate: Supply Rate = Borrow Rate × Utilization × (1 - Protocol Fee)

What assets can I use as collateral?

Supported collateral assets vary by market but typically include:

  • Stablecoins: USDC, USDT, USD1, FRAX
  • Major Cryptocurrencies: WETH, WBTC
  • DeFi Tokens: LINK, UNI, AAVE, CRV
  • LSTs: stETH, rETH, cbETH

Each asset has specific LTV ratios, liquidation thresholds, and supply caps configured by market owners.

⚡ Flash Loans & Advanced Features

How do flash loans work?

Flash loans allow you to borrow large amounts without collateral within a single transaction:

  • Borrow: Get instant access to liquidity
  • Execute: Perform your strategy (arbitrage, liquidation, etc.)
  • Repay: Return borrowed amount + fee in same transaction

Fee: Default 9 basis points (0.09%) per flash loan

Requirements: Implement IFlashLoanReceiver interface and return true from executeOperation()

💡 Use Cases: Arbitrage, liquidations, collateral swaps, debt refinancing
What is MEV protection?

Lendefi Markets includes several MEV protection mechanisms:

  • Same-block prevention: No deposit/withdraw in same block
  • Slippage protection: Maximum slippage limits on borrows
  • Price validation: Oracle price checks with deviation limits
  • Circuit breakers: Automatic pause on price anomalies

These protections help ensure fair pricing and prevent exploitative MEV extraction.

How does liquidation work?

Liquidations occur when a position's health factor falls below 1.0:

  • Trigger: Health factor < 1.0 due to collateral price drop or interest accrual
  • Liquidators: Must hold 20,000+ governance tokens
  • Process: Liquidator repays debt, receives collateral + bonus
  • Bonus: 1-4% depending on collateral tier

Health Factor: (Collateral Value × Liquidation Threshold) / Total Debt

🛡️ Security & Risk

How secure is Lendefi Markets?

Security is our top priority. Lendefi Markets includes:

  • Smart Contract Audits: Multiple professional audits
  • Open Source Code: Transparent and reviewable
  • Battle-tested Patterns: ERC-4626, OpenZeppelin contracts
  • Formal Verification: Critical functions mathematically proven
  • Bug Bounty Program: Rewards for security researchers
  • Gradual Rollout: Tested extensively on testnets
What are the main risks?

As with all DeFi protocols, there are inherent risks:

  • Smart Contract Risk: Potential bugs or exploits
  • Liquidation Risk: Collateral can be liquidated if health factor falls
  • Oracle Risk: Price feed failures or manipulation
  • Market Risk: Asset price volatility
  • Liquidity Risk: May not be able to withdraw during high utilization
⚠️ Risk Management: Never invest more than you can afford to lose. Understand liquidation mechanics and monitor your positions closely.
How are oracles secured?

Lendefi Markets uses a robust oracle system:

  • Primary: Chainlink price feeds for maximum reliability
  • Fallback: Uniswap V3 TWAP for additional validation
  • Circuit Breakers: Automatic pause on price deviations >50%
  • Volatility Checks: Reject prices with excessive movement
  • Staleness Protection: Reject outdated price data

This multi-layer approach ensures accurate and manipulation-resistant pricing.
